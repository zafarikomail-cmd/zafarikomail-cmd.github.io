Background

From Kabul
to code.

Ahmad Komail Zafari is a Full-Stack Developer and IT student at the National University of Technology (NUTECH) in Islamabad, Pakistan — originally from Afghanistan — specialising in offline-first web applications, cybersecurity tools, and community-driven software for under-resourced environments. He holds a Merit Scholarship at NUTECH and speaks English, Dari, Pashto, and Urdu.

Software as a vehicle for people who need real access, not workarounds.

I grew up in Afghanistan during a time when connectivity meant everything and infrastructure meant nothing. Watching broadcast media reach people that roads could not, I understood that technology is the most scalable form of impact.

That conviction drove me from journalism to computer science — and to build software that functions in conditions most people face: limited bandwidth, offline use, and high stakes.

Today I study Information Technology at NUTECH Islamabad on a Merit Scholarship, build full-stack systems, and explore the frontiers of AI, Robotics, and Cybersecurity. I am not here to collect credentials. I am here to build things that matter.
